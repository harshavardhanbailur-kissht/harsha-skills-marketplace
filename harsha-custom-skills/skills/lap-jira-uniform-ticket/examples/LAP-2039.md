# LAP-2039 — Move Income Considered from Yes / No selection to the Credit PD Mark as Complete form

### Problem

The Income Considered field for a co-applicant currently sits inside the Edit form of an already completed Credit PD activity. The field comes pre-defaulted to Yes because the activity is only generated for applicants and co-applicants who were marked Yes upstream. To actually set it to No, the BCM has to go through a long workaround.

### Current flow that we want to fix

1. BCM opens the Credit PD activity for the co-applicant.
2. BCM fills every income field on the form with random or placeholder values just to clear mandatory validation. These values are completely irrelevant because the co-applicant's income is not going to be used.
3. BCM clicks Mark as Complete and saves the activity.
4. BCM opens the completed Credit PD activity again in Edit mode.
5. BCM switches Income Considered from Yes to No.
6. BCM saves the form again.

This forces the BCM to fill a lot of irrelevant fields and then come back to edit the same activity just to flip one toggle. It wastes time and adds extra steps that have no business value.

### Proposed solution

1. Move the existing Income Considered field to the top of the Credit PD Mark as Complete form, as the very first field the BCM sees. No new field is being created. The default value stays Yes.
2. Behaviour on the Mark as Complete form:
    * If Income Considered stays Yes, the form works exactly like it does today. All current mandatory checks and validations apply. Save is blocked until every required field is filled correctly.
    * If the BCM switches Income Considered to No, all other fields on the form stay visible but become disabled. The BCM can save and close in one step without filling any other field. Disabled fields save as blank.
3. With this change, the BCM does not need to fill any irrelevant fields when the co-applicant's income is not going to be considered. The flow finishes in one step instead of seven.
4. Behaviour on the Edit form after completion:
    * Income Considered stays visible and editable.
    * Switching No to Yes opens every income field as editable and mandatory. The full Yes path validation runs, exactly like a fresh Mark as Complete with Yes. Save is blocked until everything is filled correctly.
    * Switching Yes to No disables every income field. Save and close is allowed in one step.

### QA Scope

1. Verify the new Mark as Complete flow for both Yes and No on a co-applicant Credit PD task.
2. Verify both directions of toggle on the Edit form save successfully.
3. After Yes to No, confirm the co-applicant shows up in the NIC (Non Income Considered) tab on the Final Sanction Pending stage OR point 4.
4. After Yes to No, confirm the same co-applicant shows up in the NIC tab on the Post Sanction stage.
5. After No to Yes, confirm the co-applicant disappears from the NIC tab on both Final Sanction Pending and Post Sanction.
6. Confirm the co-applicant still appears under Applicant Details regardless of NIC status.
7. Confirm income values in downstream views like eligibility, FOIR, and sanction letter reflect only the co-applicants currently marked Yes. (Any applicable fields for income considered yes — these are just examples.)
8. Test back to back toggles such as Yes to No to Yes to No and confirm state matches the final value at every step. (Multiple permutations and combinations.)
9. Test the NIC tab refresh behaviour extensively. Confirm whether the tab updates in real time. Verify that users at Final Sanction Pending and Post Sanction always see the correct list of co-applicants right after a toggle, without needing any manual workaround such as logout, hard refresh, or stage re-entry.

### Open Considerations for BA

The change must not affect the normal flow in any way when Income Considered stays Yes. The Yes path should look and behave exactly as it does today. This applies on screen, on save, in every automation, and at every downstream stage. Any difference on the Yes path counts as a regression. (Report it as a bug with previous expected behaviour and current behaviour.)

Items where BA needs to confirm an assumption or close a gap:

1. **Primary applicant scope.** The Credit PD activity is also generated for the primary applicant with default Yes. Allowing the BCM to set No on the primary applicant would break eligibility because LAP relies on the applicant's own income. Proposal: keep the Income Considered field visible on the primary applicant's form but lock it to Yes with no option to switch. Impact: protects eligibility calculation and prevents accidental sanction breakage.
2. **Previously filled income data on the Yes to No switch in the Edit form.** Assumption is that the prior values stay inside the activity for audit only and are disabled on screen. They do not flow downstream. Impact: clean rollback to the equivalent of a fresh No case while preserving traceability of what was filled earlier.
