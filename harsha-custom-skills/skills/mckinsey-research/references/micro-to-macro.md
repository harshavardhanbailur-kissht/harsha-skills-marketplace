# Micro-to-Macro Analysis — Multi-Level Research Pattern

## What It Is

McKinsey's signature analytical approach: examine granular company/product/case-level
data (MICRO) to illuminate broad industry/economic/societal trends (MACRO).

## How It Works — Three Levels

### Level 1: Micro (Granular Data)
- Individual company practices, decisions, performance metrics
- Specific product/technology implementations
- Case studies of success and failure
- Quantitative data at entity level (revenue, usage, adoption rates)

### Level 2: Meso (Industry/Sector Patterns)
- Aggregate patterns across companies in same sector
- Industry-wide adoption curves
- Competitive dynamics and market structure
- Regulatory environment and its effects

### Level 3: Macro (Broad Trends)
- National/global economic implications
- Societal impact patterns
- Cross-industry patterns that transcend sectors
- Policy implications and recommendations

## The Micro-to-Macro Bridge

The key insight: **Macro trends are EMERGENT PROPERTIES of micro-level decisions.**

You cannot understand macro trends by studying macro data alone. You must:
1. Collect granular micro data
2. Identify patterns that aggregate across many micro instances
3. Show HOW micro practices produce macro outcomes
4. Validate that the aggregation is statistically meaningful

### Example: McKinsey's Productivity Study
- **Micro**: Analyzed 8,300 individual firms, their practices, and performance
- **Meso**: Found sector-level patterns (e.g., tech firms vs. retail)
- **Macro**: Discovered that just 1.2% of firms drove 63% of national productivity growth
- **Insight**: National productivity is driven by a tiny number of "standout" firms

## How to Apply in Research

### For Technology Evaluation:
- **Micro**: Specific companies using the technology, their results
- **Meso**: Industry adoption patterns, competitive dynamics
- **Macro**: Market transformation, economic implications

### For Market Analysis:
- **Micro**: Individual customer behaviors, transaction data
- **Meso**: Segment-level patterns, demographic trends
- **Macro**: Market size, growth trajectory, disruption potential

### For Strategic Research:
- **Micro**: Company-level decisions that worked/failed
- **Meso**: Industry best practices emerging from many cases
- **Macro**: Strategic implications for the broader market

## Cross-Disciplinary Synthesis

McKinsey's advantage includes combining multiple disciplines:
- **Economics**: Market forces, incentives, equilibrium analysis
- **Technology/AI**: Capability assessment, adoption curves, implementation patterns
- **Behavioral Science**: Decision-making biases, change management, adoption barriers
- **Industry Expertise**: Domain-specific knowledge, regulatory context

When doing micro-to-macro analysis, always ask:
- "What does economics say about this pattern?"
- "What does behavioral science say about adoption barriers?"
- "What does technology capability say about feasibility?"
- "What does industry context say about implementation?"

## Limitations

1. **Data representativeness**: Are the micro cases representative of the whole?
   - McKinsey uses 8,300+ firms; a Claude skill uses web-available cases
   - Mitigate: Explicitly state sample limitations

2. **Survivorship bias**: Only successful cases get documented
   - Mitigate: Mandatory "failure search" (search type #4)

3. **Aggregation fallacy**: Micro patterns don't always aggregate cleanly to macro
   - Mitigate: Validate aggregation logic explicitly

4. **Causal inference**: Correlation at micro level ≠ causation at macro level
   - Mitigate: Use diagnostic evidence (ACH), not just confirmatory
